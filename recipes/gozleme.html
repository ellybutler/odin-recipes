Gözleme

Think of gözleme as Turkey's response to a quesadilla. It is a no-yeast flatbread with fillings. Simple and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 2.75 cups (400g) all-purpose flour (Note 1)
  • 1/2 tsp (3g) table salt, double if using kosher
  • 1 cup + 4 teaspoon (260ml) water
  • extra flour for shaping and rolling the dough (½ cup)
  • 4 cups (900g / 32 oz) frozen spinach, thawed and strained (Note 2)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (200g) plant-based feta (Note 3)

Directions

  1. Add flour, salt, and water into a large bowl and mix with a wooden spoon until well combined, usually takes 1-2 minutes. Hand-knead for about 10 minutes (or 4 minutes with stand-mixer on medium-speed) until the texture is no longer sticky and becomes smooth.
  2. Cover and rest for at least half an hour for the gluten to relax.
  3. The relaxed dough should minimally bounce back when poked. Divide into 8 pieces and shape them into balls using your palms. Set aside the balls until it's time to roll them out.
  4. Squeeze out most of the water from the thawed spinach and season with salt.
  5. Crumble up vegan feta and mix into the spinach.
  6. Liberally dust the work surface and rolling pin with flour and roll out each ball as thinly as you can. 1-2mm thickness is ideal. The original recipe that calls for 82g balls can be rolled into an 8-in (20-cm) diameter circle. The edges don't need to look perfect since they'll be folded in.
  7. Spread filling into the middle of the dough in a square shape. Fold all sides over like a parcel and lightly press down the edges to seal.
  8. Sprinkle flour on a cutting board or pizza paddle. Slide the gozleme onto the board to make transferring onto the cooking surface easier.
  9. Over medium-high heat, heat up ½ teaspoon of olive oil in a skillet. Get as many pans going as you have space for on the stove-top to shorten the cooking time.
  10. Place gozleme into skillet. Cook on each side for about 3-5 minutes until golden and crispy on both sides. A little charring is common.
